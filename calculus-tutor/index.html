Understanding the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

Introduction

The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus is one of the most important concepts in mathematics. It connects two big ideas:

  • Finding the area under a curve (integration)
  • Finding the rate of change (differentiation)

Part 1: The First Fundamental Theorem

This part tells us that integration and differentiation are opposite operations.

If \(F(x)\) is an antiderivative of \(f(x)\), then:

\[\int_a^b f(x)dx = F(b) - F(a)\]

Part 2: The Second Fundamental Theorem

This part shows how to find the derivative of an integral:

The derivative of a definite integral with respect to its upper limit is the integrand evaluated at that limit:

\[\frac{d}{dx}\int_a^x f(t)dt = f(x)\]

Practice Problems

If \(f(x) = x^2\), find \(\int_0^2 f(x)dx\)

If \(\frac{d}{dx}\int_0^x \sin(t)dt = \sin(x)\), what is \(\int_0^{\pi} \sin(x)dx\)?

Summary

Key Points to Remember:

  • Integration and differentiation are inverse operations
  • The area under a curve can be found using antiderivatives
  • The derivative of an integral gives back the original function
